About Me

I am currently an MS student in Northeastern's bioinformatics program, graduating May 2019. I have been working in the Lotterhos Lab at Northeastern since February 2018. I started as an unpaid volunteer and after quickly and effectively completing a few projects I was offered a position as a paid co-op. Over the past year in the Lotterhos Lab I have worked on a variety of bioinformatics projects and learned a lot about population genetics (although I still have a lot to learn!). You can see a few of my projects below, including conversion scripts for VCFs, a set of scripts used in conjunction with CellProfiler to analyze oyster larvae, and a machine learning classifier that examines a wide variety of population genetics summary statistics to find SNPs under selection or unusual rates of recombination more accurately than any of them can alone.



Before beginning my degree in bioinformatics, I attended Northeastern as an undergraduate student on a National Merit Scholarship. During that time, I worked as an environmental science teacher at an urban farm in Cape Town and as a quality assurance technician at the Sam Adams brewery before I decided that bioinformatics was the career for me. I graduated in 2017 with a BS in Biology.
